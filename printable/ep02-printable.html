Week 2: Animals — Printable Game Materials

5 days · 5 missions · Save the zoo animals!

📋 Parent Setup Guide (10 minutes)

Read this before you print!

🔦 Day 1 — Animal Footprint Tracker (Card Hunt)

Print pages 2-3 double-sided (flip on short edge). Cut out the 4 animal cards.

Hide them BEFORE your child starts the game:

🐵 Monkey card → somewhere high up (like on a shelf or top of a cabinet)

🦁 Lion card → under a blanket or cushion (the king's "den")

🐼 Panda card → on something soft and cozy (bed, sofa, or plush toy)

🐯 Tiger card → behind a door or curtain (tigers love to ambush!)

After entering the code, your child must match the footprint to the right animal. The cards spell W-I-L-D when all 4 are found.

🌈 Day 2 — Animal Pattern Puzzle

Print page 4 (pattern cards). Cut out the 3 pattern squares.

Your child will drag stripes, spots, and scales onto the right animal on screen. After the puzzle, ask them to find something at home with one of these patterns (striped shirt, spotted cup, scaly fish toy, etc.).

🎵 Day 3 — Animal Choir Rhythm

No printing needed! The game plays a sequence of animal sounds. Your child taps the animals in the same order.

Optional fun: clap or tap a steady beat while the "choir" sings. Can your child keep the rhythm?

🤸 Day 4 — Animal Shadow Mimic

Print page 5 (shadow pose cards). Cut them out and keep them nearby, or just use the on-screen shadows.

Your child copies each animal pose. You are the judge! Tap "✓ [verb] done!" when they look like the animal. Make sure there's open floor space. Join in!

❤️ Day 5 — Build an Animal Shelter (YOU are the hero!)

Important preparation:

1. Print the Heart Piece card (page 6) and keep it with you.

2. Gather household materials: a small box or basket, a tissue/towel, a bottle cap or paper bowl, crayons.

3. When the game says "Ask your Earth Helper for the Heart Piece," give your child the card. The code is 738.

4. Follow the on-screen steps to build a cozy shelter together. Then help your child describe it in English!

This is about creativity, teamwork, and caring for animals. Praise your little architect!
