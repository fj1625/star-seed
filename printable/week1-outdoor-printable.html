🌿 The Star Seed

Week 1 Outdoor — Printable Materials

2 days · 6 nature cards · 8 sounds · 10 moves · 1 seed

📋 Parent Setup Guide (15 minutes)

Read this before you head outside!

🌳 Outdoor Safety First

  • Choose a safe, familiar outdoor area: park, garden, or playground.
  • Bring water, sunscreen, a hat, and maybe a small bag for treasures.
  • Watch for cars, bodies of water, and plants you don't recognize.
  • Remind your child: look with eyes, ask before touching unknown plants.
  • Wash hands after the outdoor activities.

🌧️ Weather Backup

If it's raining or too hot, do the sound day indoors. Use the sound cards or make the noises yourselves — the wind, rain, and thunder are easy to imitate!

🔦 Day 1A — Nature Hunt (6 cards)

Print pages 2-3 double-sided (flip on short edge). Cut out the 6 cards.

Hide them BEFORE your child starts the game:

🍃 Leaf card (N) → near a bush or on the grass

🪨 Stone card (A) → on a path or near rocks

🌸 Flower card (T) → near flowers or bushes

🌿 Stick card (U) → under a tree

🪶 Feather card (R) → on open grass

🌰 Seed card (E) → near soil or a planter

The letters spell N-A-T-U-R-E. Don't tell them yet — let them discover it!

🌈 Day 1B — Nature Color Challenge

No extra printing needed, but bring the Color Reference card (page 4) on a phone or print it.

Your child will mix colors on the screen, then find real nature items that match. Encourage them to take photos of each color they find.

🎵 Day 2A — Sound Detective

Sound cards are optional (pages 5-6). The game speaks each sound. You can also make the sounds together!

🤸 Day 2B — Nature Motion Adventure

Clear a safe space outside. Join in — it's a nature dance for two!

❤️ Day 2C — Campfire Heart (YOU are the star!)

Important preparation:

1. Print the Heart Piece card (page 7) and keep it in your pocket.

2. Prepare a real seed + a small pot with soil. (Bean or sunflower seeds sprout fast!)

3. When the game says "Ask your Earth Helper for the Heart Piece," give your child the card. The secret heart code is 801.

4. At the end, plant the seed together and say: "Grow little seed, grow!"
